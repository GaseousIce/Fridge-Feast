# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

Home cooks with leftover ingredients who need meal inspiration. Primary context: standing in a kitchen, looking at an open fridge or pantry, usually on a laptop or tablet. The mood is "I have stuff but don't know what to make" — part frustration, part openness to creativity. A secondary audience is meal-preppers who plan around what needs using up.

## Product Purpose

Fridge Feast turns random leftovers into personalized, AI-generated recipes. Success looks like: a user opens the app, types in a few ingredients, and gets back a recipe that feels both achievable and exciting — reducing food waste one meal at a time.

## Positioning

The AI chef that works with what's in your fridge, so nothing goes to waste.

## Brand Personality

Warm, resourceful, playful. Voice is that of a confident home cook who's seen it all: encouraging without being cutesy, knowledgeable without being pretentious. The interface should feel like a well-loved cookbook meets a clever friend who always knows what to make with three random vegetables and an egg.

## Anti-references

- Sterile white meal-planning dashboards (EveryPlate, Mealime)
- Dark, serious "pro chef" recipe apps
- The generic SaaS card-grid + sidebar pattern (not a workspace tool)
- Gradient text or glassmorphism "AI" aesthetics
- Overly cutesy or cartoonish kitchen UIs

## Design Principles

- **Ingredients first.** The input is the hero. Everything else supports it. The recipe result is reward, not the default state.
- **Kitchen companion, not dashboard.** Warm but not cutesy. The tone of a well-worn cookbook, not a productivity tool.
- **Confidence in every recipe.** The output must feel achievable, not intimidating. Clear structure, realistic cook times, no chef jargon.
- **Delight through motion, not decoration.** Purposeful animation (theme toggle, recipe reveal, loading) that feels tactile and responsive — but never decorative for its own sake.
- **Reduce friction to creation.** Fewer fields, smarter defaults, faster results. The gap between "what's in my fridge" and "what's for dinner" should be as short as possible.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA minimum. Screen-reader-friendly live regions for the generation process. Keyboard-navigable form (Cmd+Enter to generate). Reduced motion respected for transitions and reveal animations.
